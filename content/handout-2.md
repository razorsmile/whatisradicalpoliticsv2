## Essential Terms for Understanding Radical Traditions

<dl>
<dt><strong>Autonomy</strong></dt>
<dd>Self-governance independent of state control</dd>

<dt><strong>Capitalism</strong></dt>
<dd>Economic system where means of production are privately owned and operated for profit</dd>

<dt><strong>Circulation of Struggles</strong></dt>
<dd>How movements in different places/sectors influence each other</dd>

<dt><strong>Civil Society</strong></dt>
<dd>Organizations and relationships outside state and market</dd>

<dt><strong>Class</strong></dt>
<dd>Your relationship to the means of production (do you own them or work for those who do?)</dd>

<dt><strong>Collective Memory</strong></dt>
<dd>Shared understanding of the past</dd>

<dt><strong>Colonialism</strong></dt>
<dd>System of domination where one nation controls another's land, resources, and people</dd>

<dt><strong>Commons</strong></dt>
<dd>Resources governed collectively, neither private nor state property</dd>

<dt><strong>Composition</strong></dt>
<dd>The technical, social, and political organization of the working class</dd>

<dt><strong>Counter-Narrative</strong></dt>
<dd>Alternative to dominant historical accounts</dd>

<dt><strong>Counter-Power</strong></dt>
<dd>Collective capacity to refuse domination and create alternatives</dd>

<dt><strong>Dual Power</strong></dt>
<dd>Building alternative institutions alongside existing ones</dd>

<dt><strong>Hidden Transcript</strong></dt>
<dd>Resistance practices invisible to power</dd>

<dt><strong>Historiography</strong></dt>
<dd>The study of how history is written</dd>

<dt><strong>Intersectionality</strong></dt>
<dd>How different systems of oppression (race, class, gender) overlap and interact</dd>

<dt><strong>Means of Production</strong></dt>
<dd>Tools, factories, land, and resources used to produce goods</dd>

<dt><strong>The Multitude</strong></dt>
<dd>Diverse formation of all who labor under capitalism</dd>

<dt><strong>Mutual Aid</strong></dt>
<dd>Voluntary reciprocal exchange of resources and services for mutual benefit</dd>

<dt><strong>Oral History</strong></dt>
<dd>Historical method using interviews and testimony</dd>

<dt><strong>Patriarchy</strong></dt>
<dd>System of male dominance in social, political, and economic life</dd>

<dt><strong>Prefigurative Politics</strong></dt>
<dd>Creating the world you want to see in how you organize now</dd>

<dt><strong>Praxis</strong></dt>
<dd>Unity of theory and practice - ideas tested through action</dd>

<dt><strong>Recuperation</strong></dt>
<dd>System's ability to absorb and neutralize radical challenges</dd>

<dt><strong>Social Reproduction</strong></dt>
<dd>Work required to maintain life - cooking, cleaning, caring, raising children</dd>

<dt><strong>Solidarity</strong></dt>
<dd>Unity based on shared interests and mutual support</dd>

<dt><strong>The State</strong></dt>
<dd>Government institutions plus military, police, courts, bureaucracy</dd>

<dt><strong>Workers' Inquiry (Conricerca)</strong></dt>
<dd>Research methodology where workers investigate their own conditions</dd>
</dl>
