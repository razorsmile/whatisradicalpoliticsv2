HANDOUT 1: Course Overview & Expectations

Welcome!

This four-week course explores radical politics through four academic lenses, providing you with analytical tools for understanding and engaging with radical political movements.

What to Expect

  • No mandatory homework - all tasks are optional
  • Experience valued - your knowledge matters as much as any text
  • Disagreement welcome - tensions between ideas are productive
  • Local focus - everything relates to Brighton and our lives here

Schedule

Week 1 (Philosophy): What Makes Politics Radical?

Week 2 (Political Theory): In, Against, and Beyond the State

Week 3 (History): Radical Brighton - Past and Present

Week 4 (Sociology): Mapping Working-Class Counter-Power

Optional Engagement

Each week offers optional tasks for going deeper. These might include:

  • Researching local history
  • Identifying radical projects
  • Reading suggested texts
  • Contributing to collective mapping

Ground Rules

  • What's shared here stays here (unless someone explicitly agrees otherwise)
  • Step up/step back - make space for all voices
  • Critique ideas, not people
  • Start from curiosity, not judgment
