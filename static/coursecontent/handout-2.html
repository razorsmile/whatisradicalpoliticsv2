HANDOUT 3: Six Radical Traditions Summary

1. SOCIALISM

Root Problem: Private ownership of means of production

Solution: Collective ownership, democratic planning

Brighton Example: Campaigns for council housing

2. ANARCHISM

Root Problem: Hierarchy and coercive authority

Solution: Horizontal organization, mutual aid

Brighton Example: The Cowley Club

3. COMMUNISM

Root Problem: Class society

Solution: Classless, stateless society

Brighton Example: 1930s Unemployed Workers Movement

4. FEMINISM (Radical)

Root Problem: Patriarchy intersecting with capitalism/racism

Solution: Transform gendered power relations

Brighton Example: Reclaim the Night marches

5. ANTI-RACIST/ANTI-COLONIAL

Root Problem: Colonial relations and their legacies

Solution: Decolonize knowledge, institutions, relationships

Brighton Example: Palestine Solidarity Campaign

6. ECOLOGICAL POLITICS (Radical)

Root Problem: Exploitation of nature linked to exploitation of people

Solution: Transform human-nature relationship

Brighton Example: Anti-fracking resistance

Key Insight

These traditions are in conversation and tension with each other - most movements draw from multiple traditions.
