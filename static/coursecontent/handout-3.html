HANDOUT 2: Jargon Buster

Essential Terms for Understanding Radical Traditions

Capitalism
Economic system where means of production are privately owned and operated for profit
Class
Your relationship to the means of production (do you own them or work for those who do?)
Colonialism
System of domination where one nation controls another's land, resources, and people
Dual Power
Building alternative institutions alongside existing ones
Counter-Power
Power that exists outside of the state and is used to resist the state
Intersectionality
How different systems of oppression (race, class, gender) overlap and interact
Means of Production
Tools, factories, land, and resources used to produce goods
Mutual Aid
Voluntary reciprocal exchange of resources and services for mutual benefit
Patriarchy
System of male dominance in social, political, and economic life
Prefigurative Politics
Creating the world you want to see in how you organize now
Praxis
Unity of theory and practice - ideas tested through action
Social Reproduction
Work required to maintain life - cooking, cleaning, caring, raising children
Solidarity
Unity based on shared interests and mutual support
