HANDOUT 6: Researching Radical History

Where to Look

Official Archives (read against the grain):

  • The Keep (East Sussex Records Office)
  • Brighton Museum & Art Gallery
  • Brighton History Centre (Library)

Alternative Sources:

  • QueenSpark Books (oral histories)
  • Union archives and records
  • Radical newspapers (Brighton Voice, etc.)
  • Personal collections and family stories

Questions to Ask

  1. Whose voices are missing from official accounts?
  2. What patterns of resistance existed?
  3. How did ordinary people organize?
  4. What succeeded and what failed? Why?
  5. What can we learn and adapt today?

Methods

  • Oral History: Interview participants while alive
  • Place-Based: What happened on your street?
  • Counter-Reading: What do hostile sources reveal?
  • Pattern Seeking: Look for recurring tactics/strategies

  • 1970s: Squatting movement peaks
